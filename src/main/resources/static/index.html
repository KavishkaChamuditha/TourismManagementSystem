Tourist Safty

  • Before traveling, register with your country's embassy or consulate in Sri Lanka. This allows them to contact you in case of an emergency and provide assistance if needed.

  • Carry the contact details of your embassy or consulate with you at all times. In case of an emergency, they can provide guidance, support, and help with any consular services you may require.

  • Be cautious of your surroundings: Stay aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded places, tourist areas, and public transportation. Keep an eye on your belongings and be cautious of pickpockets. Avoid displaying expensive items or large amounts of cash in public.

  • Choose registered taxis, reputable car rental services, or arrange transportation through your accommodation. If using public transportation, be cautious of overcrowding and keep an eye on your belongings.

  • Keep yourself informed about the local news and any potential political or social unrest in Sri Lanka. Avoid areas that are known to be unstable or prone to conflicts.

Get your tickets

  • Upon arrival in Sri Lanka, you will need to go through immigration and present your passport and visa (if applicable). Follow the instructions of the immigration officers and answer their questions honestly.

  • Sri Lanka has a rich cultural heritage, and it's important to respect the local customs and traditions. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites, and be mindful of cultural sensitivities.

  • Avoid taking photographs or videos of sensitive government buildings, military installations, or personnel. It's always a good practice to seek permission before photographing individuals, especially in rural or traditional areas.

  • Sri Lanka is known for its diverse wildlife and natural beauty. Respect protected areas, national parks, and wildlife reserves by following designated trails, not disturbing or feeding animals, and avoiding littering.
